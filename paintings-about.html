1. Understanding Color Theory

Color theory is a fundamental concept in painting. Learn about primary, secondary, and tertiary colors, how to mix them, and how complementary colors can add contrast and vibrancy to your work.

2. Choosing the Right Brushes

Flat, round, filbert, fan, and angle brushes all have different uses. Know when to use each one based on your painting technique and desired texture.

3. Mastering Brush Strokes

Practice various brush strokes like dabbing, scumbling, stippling, and dry brushing to create different effects and layers in your painting.

4. Setting Up Your Workspace

A clean, organized, and well-lit workspace can significantly enhance your focus and efficiency while painting. Make sure to have all your materials easily accessible.

5. Protecting and Preserving Your Art

Use fixatives, varnishes, or protective sprays based on the medium you use. Store your paintings in a dry, cool place to avoid color fading or canvas damage.
